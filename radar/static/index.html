Each hub is a root-cause cluster; each satellite is a post, colored by platform. Only 4 hues are safely distinguishable and clear of the red/orange/green already used for risk rings and status, so Reddit/YouTube share a hue (circle/diamond) and Hacker News/X share another -- shape, not color, tells those two pairs apart (Mastodon is a square). Solid satellites are scored alerts, sized by velocity; hollow satellites are classified pain points that haven't crossed the velocity threshold (or don't have enough snapshot history) yet. A hub with satellites in more than one color -- alerted or not -- is the same issue showing up on more than one platform, a bigger footprint than any single post's size or status suggests. An orange ring means a post has crossed into med-severity ("danger") territory; a red ring means high severity ("full risk") -- independent of platform color, and absent entirely for low severity so a calm post stays uncluttered. A flashing red outer ring means that alert's incident is still "open" -- nobody has acknowledged or started working it yet (see the Alerts tab); it stops flashing the moment someone does. Click a hub to see its protection tier (Settings > Model protection) -- a flagship-model root cause is also flagged in the Root-cause clusters chart below. Drag any node to reposition it; click for a persistent detail card instead of a hover tooltip; uncheck a platform or category above to hide it (double-click one to isolate it), or use the Client filter to isolate one watched client's posts. The legend below is a color key only -- filtering lives above the graph, not split across both ends of it.

Escalation criteria

Per-category: whether it requires human QA before an alert is trusted, an optional velocity-threshold override (blank = use the platform/global default), and a first-response playbook. Changes here affect the next radar score run, CLI or dashboard-triggered.
